Class 12 CBSE Political Science Q&A (2024-25)

Simple and Easy Revision Guide

Part A: Contemporary World Politics

1. What was the Cold War?

The Cold War was a long period of tension between the United States (USA) and the Soviet Union (USSR) after World War II. They didn’t fight directly but competed through weapons, space races, and influence over other countries. It lasted from 1945 to 1991.

2. Why did the Soviet Union break up?

The Soviet Union broke up in 1991 because of economic problems, political corruption, and demands for freedom from its republics like Russia, Ukraine, and others. People were unhappy with the strict government, and leaders couldn’t fix the issues.

3. What is meant by ‘Shock Therapy’?

Shock Therapy was a sudden change in ex-Soviet countries after 1991. They shifted from a government-controlled economy to a free-market economy quickly. It caused hardships like price rise and unemployment.

4. What is the role of the United Nations (UN)?

The UN works to keep peace in the world, solve conflicts between countries, and help with problems like poverty, health, and human rights. It’s like a global team for cooperation.

5. What is globalization?

Globalization means the world becoming more connected through trade, technology, and culture. It’s when countries share goods, ideas, and services easily, like buying clothes from another country online.

Part B: Politics in India Since Independence

6. What were the main challenges India faced after independence?

India faced three big challenges:
- Partition: Violence and migration due to the split into India and Pakistan.
- Unity: Bringing 500+ princely states into one nation.
- Development: Fighting poverty and building a strong economy.

7. Why was the first election in India important?

The first election in 1951-52 was important because it showed India’s commitment to democracy. Everyone, even illiterate people, got to vote for their leaders, making it a huge success.

8. What was the Emergency in India (1975-77)?

The Emergency was when PM Indira Gandhi suspended democracy from June 25, 1975, to 1977. She jailed opponents and controlled the press because she said the country was in danger. It was controversial.

9. What is the Mandal Commission?

The Mandal Commission (1980) suggested giving 27% reservation in government jobs and education to backward classes (OBCs). It caused protests but aimed to help weaker sections.

10. What is coalition government in India?

A coalition government is when many political parties join together to rule because no single party gets a majority in elections. It started happening more in India after the 1980s.

Additional Key Questions

11. What is the difference between traditional and non-traditional security?

- Traditional Security: Focuses on military threats, like war or invasion.
- Non-Traditional Security: Focuses on non-military issues, like climate change, terrorism, or pandemics.

12. Why did India choose a secular state?

India chose to be secular to treat all religions equally and avoid conflicts in a diverse country with Hindus, Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, and others living together.

13. What was the Green Revolution?

The Green Revolution (1960s-70s) was when India used new farming methods, seeds, and fertilizers to grow more food, especially wheat and rice. It helped reduce hunger but caused some environmental issues.

14. What is regionalism in India?

Regionalism is when people focus on their state or region’s needs over the nation’s, like demands for separate states (e.g., Telangana) or more power for local languages.

15. How has globalization affected India?

Globalization brought more jobs, technology, and foreign companies to India (like IT and mobile brands). But it also increased inequality and harmed small farmers and businesses.
