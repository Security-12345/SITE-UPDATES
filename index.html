Class 12 CBSE Political Science Notes

2024-25 Syllabus | Simple & Easy | By Jagroop Singh

Part A: Contemporary World Politics

Chapter 1: The End of Bipolarity

  • Soviet Union broke in 1991—Cold War ended.
  • Why? Economic mess, weak leaders, freedom demands.
  • Shock Therapy: Fast switch to capitalism—chaos followed.
  • USA became the only superpower; Russia rose.
  • India stayed buddies with Russia.
Key Idea: World went from two powers to one!

Chapter 2: New Centres of Power

  • New strong groups grew after USSR fell.
  • EU: Europe teamed up for trade.
  • ASEAN: Southeast Asia joined for peace.
  • China: Big in money and military.
  • India works with all these groups.
Key Idea: Power isn’t just USA’s anymore!

Chapter 3: Contemporary South Asia

  • Countries: India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, etc.
  • Issues: Fights (Kashmir), poverty, weak democracy.
  • SAARC: Group for trade and peace.
  • India leads but has tensions (Pakistan).
Key Idea: South Asia fights but tries teamwork.

Chapter 4: International Organisations

  • UN: Countries solve issues together.
  • UN Jobs: Peace, poverty, climate.
  • Security Council: 5 big countries control.
  • Issue: UN’s old—India wants a bigger role.
Key Idea: UN helps but needs fixing.

Chapter 5: Security in the Contemporary World

  • Security: Safe from war, terror, etc.
  • Old: Wars. New: Terror, cyber, diseases.
  • Solutions: Teamwork or fight.
  • India: Faces Pakistan, China, terror.
Key Idea: Safety’s more than just wars now.

Chapter 6: Environment and Natural Resources

  • Problems: Pollution, climate, resource fights.
  • Deals: Paris Agreement to save Earth.
  • India: Grows but goes green (solar).
  • Rich countries use more, blame others.
Key Idea: Earth needs help—who does it?

Chapter 7: Globalisation

  • World connects—trade, culture, tech.
  • Good: Jobs, products. Bad: Big wins, small lose.
  • India: Opened in 1991—growth + struggles.
Key Idea: World’s smaller, not always fair.

Part B: Politics in India Since Independence

Chapter 1: Challenges of Nation-Building

  • 1947: India free but messy.
  • Partition: India-Pakistan split, chaos.
  • Unity: 500+ states joined India.
  • Diversity: Many languages, cultures.
Key Idea: Building India was tough but worked.

Chapter 2: Planned Development

  • Plans to grow after 1947.
  • Five-Year Plans: Farms, factories.
  • Goals: Less poverty, more jobs.
  • Some wins, some still poor.
Key Idea: India planned its future.

Chapter 3: India’s Foreign Policy

  • Non-Alignment: No USA or USSR side.
  • Wars: Pakistan (1947, 65, 71), China (62).
  • Today: Friends with USA, Russia.
Key Idea: India stays independent globally.

Chapter 4: Parties and the Party System in India

  • Congress ruled early years.
  • 1977: Others won, coalitions started.
  • Now: BJP, Congress, regional parties.
Key Idea: From one party to many.

Chapter 5: Democratic Resurgence

  • Emergency (1975-77): Freedoms stopped.
  • People fought back—more parties.
  • Today: Big, messy elections work.
Key Idea: Democracy bounced back.

Chapter 6: Regional Aspirations

  • States wanted freedom: Kashmir, Punjab.
  • Northeast: Identity fights.
  • India gave some rights, kept unity.
Key Idea: Balancing local demands.

Chapter 7: Indian Politics: Trends and Developments

  • Coalitions: No single ruler.
  • Religion, caste in votes.
  • BJP rose, welfare schemes big.
Key Idea: Politics now diverse, competitive.
