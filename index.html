Short Answers (2 & 4 Marks)

7. Why did the Soviet Union fall? (2 Marks)

The Soviet Union fell because it ran out of money to keep up with the USA in the arms race. Also, Gorbachev’s changes—like opening up (Glasnost) and fixing the economy (Perestroika)—made people want freedom, and it broke apart in 1991.

8. What is globalization in easy words? (2 Marks)

Globalization means the world getting connected—like sharing goods, ideas, and tech across countries. It’s about less borders and more trade, like buying phones from China or watching Hollywood movies in India.

9. What happened in Russia after Shock Therapy? (4 Marks)

Shock Therapy was when Russia quickly changed from a government-run economy to a free market after 1991. It caused big problems: Factories shut down, people lost jobs. Prices shot up, money became worthless. Rich guys (oligarchs) got super rich, most people got poor. No more free healthcare or help—life got tough.

10. What problems did India face right after 1947? (4 Marks)

After 1947, India had big challenges: Partition Mess—Millions moved, fights broke out, like Hindu-Muslim riots. Princely States—500+ states had to join India, some like Hyderabad didn’t want to. Poverty—Most people were poor, no food or jobs. Unity—Different languages and cultures had to stick together.

Long Answers (6 Marks)

11. How has China’s rise changed the world? Explain simply. (6 Marks)

China’s rise has shaken up the world in big ways: Money Power—China’s economy grew fast—it makes stuff cheap and sells everywhere. Projects like Belt and Road help it control trade. Army Power—China’s building a strong military—like ships in the South China Sea—making neighbors like India nervous (think 2020 border fights). World Voice—As a UN Security Council member, it says no to things USA wants. India’s Side—We trade a lot with China but also argue over borders. It’s making the world less about just the USA and more about many powers.

12. Was the Emergency in 1975 good or bad for India? Explain simply. (6 Marks)

The Emergency (1975-77) happened when Indira Gandhi got scared after a court said she cheated in elections. Here’s what happened: Bad Stuff—She stopped democracy—jailed leaders, shut newspapers, and forced rules like sterilization. People lost freedom. Good Stuff—Trains ran on time, government worked faster, and some poor got help. Long Run—It hurt trust in Congress, but people fought back, ending it in 1977 with elections. It showed democracy can bounce back, but it was mostly bad for rights.
