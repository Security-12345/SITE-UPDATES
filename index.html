CBSE Class 12 Political Science 2025

For Anmol Singh

Section A: MCQs (1 Mark Each)

Q1. What was the main reason for the end of the Cold War?

a) Economic collapse of the Soviet Union b) World War III c) Formation of NATO d) Rise of China

Answer: a) Economic collapse of the Soviet Union

Explanation: The Cold War ended mainly because the Soviet Union faced economic problems and could not compete with the USA, leading to its breakup in 1991.

Q2. Which country is not a permanent member of the UN Security Council?

a) USA b) Russia c) India d) China

Answer: c) India

Explanation: The permanent members are USA, Russia, UK, France, and China. India is not a permanent member but wants to be.

Q3. What does ‘ASEAN’ stand for?

a) Association of Southeast Asian Nations b) Alliance of South European Nations c) African Social Economic Agreement d) Asian Security and Economic Network

Answer: a) Association of Southeast Asian Nations

Explanation: ASEAN is a group of countries in Southeast Asia working together for peace and growth.

Q4. Who was the first Prime Minister of India?

a) Jawaharlal Nehru b) Mahatma Gandhi c) Sardar Patel d) Indira Gandhi

Answer: a) Jawaharlal Nehru

Explanation: Jawaharlal Nehru became India’s first PM in 1947 after independence.

Q5. The ‘Two-Nation Theory’ was given by:

a) Mahatma Gandhi b) Muhammad Ali Jinnah c) Jawaharlal Nehru d) B.R. Ambedkar

Answer: b) Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Explanation: Jinnah proposed that Hindus and Muslims are two separate nations, leading to Pakistan’s creation.

Q6. Which article of the Indian Constitution gives special status to Jammu and Kashmir (before 2019)?

a) Article 370 b) Article 356 c) Article 14 d) Article 21

Answer: a) Article 370

Explanation: Article 370 gave special rights to Jammu and Kashmir, like its own constitution, until it was removed in 2019.

Q7. The ‘Green Revolution’ in India was related to:

a) Forest conservation b) Increase in agriculture production c) Water management d) Industrial growth

Answer: b) Increase in agriculture production

Explanation: The Green Revolution used new seeds and technology to grow more food in the 1960s and 70s.

Q8. What is the full form of SAARC?

a) South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation b) South African Alliance for Regional Control c) Southeast Asian Agreement for Regional Cooperation d) South American Association for Rural Cooperation

Answer: a) South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation

Explanation: SAARC is a group of South Asian countries like India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh working together.

Q9. The Emergency in India was declared in:

a) 1962 b) 1971 c) 1975 d) 1980

Answer: c) 1975

Explanation: Indira Gandhi declared Emergency in 1975 due to political problems, lasting until 1977.

Q10. Which movement was led by Sardar Patel to unite princely states?

a) Quit India Movement b) Integration of Princely States c) Dandi Salt March d) Non-Cooperation Movement

Answer: b) Integration of Princely States

Explanation: Sardar Patel worked to bring over 500 princely states into India after 1947.

Q11. The policy of ‘Non-Alignment’ means:

a) Supporting USA in the Cold War b) Not joining any power bloc c) Fighting against colonialism d) Joining the Soviet Union

Answer: b) Not joining any power bloc

Explanation: India stayed neutral during the Cold War, not siding with the USA or Soviet Union.

Q12. Globalization means:

a) More trade and connection between countries b) Closing borders c) Reducing technology use d) Stopping migration

Answer: a) More trade and connection between countries

Explanation: Globalization connects the world through trade, culture, and technology.

Section B: Very Short Answer (2 Marks Each)

Q13. What is meant by ‘Unipolar World’?

Answer: A unipolar world means one country, like the USA, is the most powerful in the world after the Cold War ended.

Explanation: After the Soviet Union broke up in 1991, the USA became the only superpower.

Q14. Name two leaders of the Non-Aligned Movement.

Answer: Jawaharlal Nehru (India) and Josip Broz Tito (Yugoslavia).

Explanation: They started the Non-Aligned Movement to stay neutral in the Cold War.

Q15. What is the main goal of the United Nations?

Answer: The main goal of the UN is to maintain peace and security in the world.

Explanation: It was formed in 1945 to stop wars and help countries work together.

Q16. What was the ‘Partition of India’?

Answer: The Partition of India was the division of British India into India and Pakistan in 1947 based on religion.

Explanation: Hindus got India, and Muslims got Pakistan, causing migration and violence.

Q17. What is a ‘Coalition Government’?

Answer: A coalition government is when two or more political parties join to form a government.

Explanation: This happens when no single party gets enough seats to rule alone.

Q18. Define ‘Secularism’ in the Indian Constitution.

Answer: Secularism means the government treats all religions equally and does not favor any one religion.

Explanation: India allows freedom of religion for all citizens.

Section C: Short Answer (4 Marks Each)

Q19. Explain the role of the Soviet Union in the Cold War.

Answer: The Soviet Union was one of the two superpowers in the Cold War, competing with the USA. It led the communist countries and formed the Warsaw Pact to fight against NATO. It supported countries like Cuba and Vietnam against the USA. The Cold War ended when the Soviet Union broke up in 1991 due to economic failure.

Explanation: The Soviet Union and USA never fought directly but had a rivalry through wars, spies, and technology.

Q20. What are the main features of India’s foreign policy?

Answer: India’s foreign policy has these features:

1. Non-Alignment: Not joining any superpower during the Cold War.

2. Peace: Promoting peace and solving disputes without war.

3. Friendship: Building good relations with all countries.

4. Support to UN: Working with the UN for global peace.

Explanation: India follows these ideas to stay independent and peaceful in the world.

Q21. Why did India adopt a mixed economy after independence?

Answer: India adopted a mixed economy because:

1. It wanted both government and private companies to grow the economy.

2. It aimed to reduce poverty and inequality using government control.

3. It needed fast development after British rule ended.

4. It balanced socialism (public sector) and capitalism (private sector).

Explanation: This system helped India build industries and support the poor.

Q22. What were the effects of the Emergency (1975–77) in India?

Answer: Effects of the Emergency were:

1. Loss of Freedom: People lost rights like speech and press.

2. Arrests: Many leaders and citizens were jailed without reason.

3. Fear: People were scared of the government’s strict rules.

4. Elections: After it ended, Indira Gandhi lost the 1977 election.

Explanation: The Emergency was a tough time for democracy in India.

Q23. How does globalization affect India?

Answer: Globalization affects India in these ways:

1. Economy: More jobs and trade with other countries.

2. Technology: Better phones, internet, and machines.

3. Culture: Mixing of Indian and foreign cultures (e.g., food, movies).

4. Problems: Some local businesses and farmers suffer.

Explanation: Globalization has both good and bad impacts on India.

Section E: Long Answer (6 Marks Each)

Q27. Discuss the challenges faced by India during partition in 1947.

Answer: India faced these challenges during partition:

1. Violence: Riots between Hindus and Muslims killed many people.

2. Migration: Millions moved between India and Pakistan, leaving homes behind.

3. Refugees: The government had to help settle displaced people.

4. Princely States: Some states like Hyderabad and Kashmir didn’t join India easily.

5. Economy: Division of resources weakened the economy.

6. Unity: Leaders worked hard to keep India united despite division.

Explanation: Partition was a tough time, but leaders like Gandhi and Patel solved many issues.

Q28. Explain the role of the Election Commission in India.

Answer: The Election Commission (EC) has these roles:

1. Conducting Elections: It organizes free and fair elections for Parliament and state assemblies.

2. Voter List: Prepares and updates the list of voters.

3. Rules: Makes rules to stop cheating, like spending limits.

4. Monitoring: Watches elections to ensure they are honest.

5. Symbols: Gives symbols to political parties.

6. Education: Teaches people about voting rights.

Explanation: The EC keeps democracy strong by making elections fair.

Q29. What are the advantages and disadvantages of coalition governments in India?

Answer:

Advantages:

1. More Voices: Many parties get to share ideas and rule together.

2. Balance: Stops one party from having too much power.

3. Representation: Small groups and regions get heard.

Disadvantages:

1. Instability: Governments can fall if parties fight.

2. Slow Decisions: Agreement takes time, delaying work.

3. Compromise: Policies may weaken to please everyone.

Explanation: Coalition governments are common in India but can be strong or weak depending on teamwork.

Q30. How has India contributed to world peace?

Answer: India has helped world peace by:

1. Non-Alignment: Stayed neutral in the Cold War, reducing tension.

2. UN Peacekeeping: Sent soldiers to UN missions in Congo, Sudan, etc.

3. No War Policy: Solved disputes with talks, not fights (e.g., with Pakistan).

4. Support to Freedom: Helped countries like South Africa fight colonialism.

5. Global Groups: Active in UN, NAM, and SAARC for peace.

6. Gandhi’s Ideas: Spread non-violence worldwide.

Explanation: India’s peaceful approach makes it a respected country globally.
