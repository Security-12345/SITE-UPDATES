Political Science Exam Prep - Class 12 CBSE

1. (MCQ) Which of the following countries is a permanent member of the UN Security Council?
c) China
Explanation: The five permanent members of the UN Security Council are the United States, Russia, China, France, and the United Kingdom.
2. (2 Marks) What were the main reasons for the end of the Cold War?
The Cold War ended due to:
- The economic and military exhaustion of the Soviet Union, which could no longer sustain the arms race with the USA.
- Internal reforms like Gorbachev’s Glasnost (openness) and Perestroika (restructuring), which weakened the Soviet system and led to its disintegration by 1991.
3. (4 Marks) Explain the concept of ‘Shock Therapy’ and its impact on Russia.
Shock Therapy refers to the sudden transition from a socialist to a capitalist economy in post-Soviet states after 1991. In Russia, it involved rapid privatization and market liberalization. Its impacts included:
- Economic collapse, with industries disappearing and inflation soaring.
- Increased poverty and inequality, creating a divide between rich oligarchs and the poor.
- Weakened state control, leading to corruption and instability.
- Loss of social welfare systems, making life harder for citizens.
4. (6 Marks) “The rise of China has reshaped global politics.” Justify this statement with suitable arguments.
China’s rise has transformed global politics through:
- Economic Power: Its rapid economic growth and initiatives like the Belt and Road Initiative have increased its global influence, challenging Western dominance.
- Military Strength: Expansion of its military, especially in the South China Sea, has shifted power dynamics in Asia.
- Diplomatic Influence: China’s role in international organizations and veto power in the UN Security Council amplify its voice.
- India’s Perspective: India faces border tensions (e.g., Galwan 2020) but also cooperates economically, reflecting a complex relationship.
This multipolar shift reduces US unipolarity, reshaping global alliances.
5. (MCQ) Which event led to the imposition of the National Emergency in India on June 25, 1975?
b) Allahabad High Court verdict
Explanation: The Emergency was declared after the Allahabad High Court invalidated Indira Gandhi’s election in 1975, triggering political unrest.
6. (2 Marks) Briefly explain the Mandal Issue and one reason for the anti-Mandal protests.
The Mandal Issue refers to the 1990 decision to implement the Mandal Commission’s recommendation, reserving 27% of government jobs for Other Backward Classes (OBCs). One reason for anti-Mandal protests was the fear among upper-caste students that it would reduce their job opportunities.
7. (4 Marks) What were the challenges faced by the Election Commission during India’s first general elections?
The Election Commission faced:
- Illiteracy: Most voters couldn’t read, so symbols were used for parties.
- Logistical Issues: Organizing polling across a vast, diverse country with poor infrastructure was daunting.
- Voter Registration: Creating an electoral roll for millions, many without documentation, was complex.
- Awareness: Educating people about voting in a new democracy was a significant task.
8. (6 Marks) “Regional demands in India exemplify the principle of unity in diversity.” Do you agree? Support with arguments.
Yes, I agree. Regional demands reflect India’s diversity while reinforcing unity:
- Linguistic States: Formation of states like Andhra Pradesh (1953) based on language preserved cultural identity within a united India.
- Northeast Autonomy: Granting autonomy to states like Nagaland balances regional aspirations with national integrity.
- Federal Structure: Power-sharing through federalism accommodates diverse demands (e.g., Tamil Nadu’s language protests) without breaking unity.
However, excessive demands (e.g., separatism in Kashmir) sometimes challenge unity, though resolved through dialogue, showing India’s strength in managing diversity.
