CBSE Class 12 Political Science Practice 2025

Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (1 Mark Each)

Q1. Which event marked the beginning of the end of the Cold War?

a) Fall of the Berlin Wall
b) Formation of NATO
c) Cuban Missile Crisis
d) Korean War
Answer: a) Fall of the Berlin Wall
Explanation: The Berlin Wall fell in 1989, showing the collapse of Soviet control and the end of the Cold War.

Q2. India’s ‘Act East Policy’ focuses on improving ties with:

a) European Union
b) Southeast Asian countries
c) Middle Eastern countries
d) African nations
Answer: b) Southeast Asian countries
Explanation: Started in 2014, this policy strengthens India’s relations with ASEAN and East Asian countries.

Q3. Which organization was formed to promote peace after World War II?

a) NATO
b) United Nations
c) ASEAN
d) SAARC
Answer: b) United Nations
Explanation: The UN was created in 1945 to maintain global peace and cooperation.

Q4. Who led the integration of princely states into India?

a) Jawaharlal Nehru
b) Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel
c) Mahatma Gandhi
d) Indira Gandhi
Answer: b) Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel
Explanation: Patel united over 500 princely states into India after 1947.

Q5. The ‘Panchsheel Agreement’ was signed between India and:

a) Pakistan
b) China
c) USA
d) Soviet Union
Answer: b) China
Explanation: Signed in 1954, it aimed for peace and cooperation but later weakened due to the 1962 war.

Q6. Which amendment made education a fundamental right in India?

a) 42nd Amendment
b) 86th Amendment
c) 44th Amendment
d) 73rd Amendment
Answer: b) 86th Amendment
Explanation: Passed in 2002, it added Article 21A, making education free for children aged 6–14.

Q7. The ‘Look East Policy’ was introduced by which Prime Minister?

a) Narasimha Rao
b) Rajiv Gandhi
c) Atal Bihari Vajpayee
d) Manmohan Singh
Answer: a) Narasimha Rao
Explanation: Started in 1991, it aimed to connect India with Southeast Asia.

Q8. Which country is India’s biggest trade partner in SAARC?

a) Pakistan
b) Bangladesh
c) Sri Lanka
d) Nepal
Answer: b) Bangladesh
Explanation: India and Bangladesh share strong trade ties in South Asia.

Q9. The Mandal Commission recommended reservations for:

a) Scheduled Castes
b) Other Backward Classes
c) Scheduled Tribes
d) Women
Answer: b) Other Backward Classes
Explanation: In 1990, it suggested 27% reservation for OBCs in government jobs.

Q10. Which war led to the creation of Bangladesh?

a) 1962 India-China War
b) 1965 India-Pakistan War
c) 1971 India-Pakistan War
d) Kargil War
Answer: c) 1971 India-Pakistan War
Explanation: India helped East Pakistan become Bangladesh in 1971.

Q11. The term ‘BRICS’ includes which of these countries?

a) Britain
b) Brazil
c) Bangladesh
d) Belgium
Answer: b) Brazil
Explanation: BRICS stands for Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa.

Q12. What is the main source of energy in India’s ‘Energy Security’ policy?

a) Solar power
b) Coal
c) Wind power
d) Nuclear power
Answer: b) Coal
Explanation: Coal remains India’s biggest energy source, though renewable energy is growing.

Section B: Very Short Answer Questions (2 Marks Each)

Q13. What is meant by ‘Multipolar World’?

Answer: A multipolar world means many countries, like USA, China, and India, share global power instead of one dominating.
Explanation: After the Cold War, power shifted from just USA to multiple nations.

Q14. Name two objectives of India’s foreign policy.

Answer: 1. Promote peace. 2. Build economic ties with other countries.
Explanation: India wants a peaceful world and growth through trade.

Q15. What was the ‘Nehru-Liaquat Pact’?

Answer: It was an agreement in 1950 between India and Pakistan to protect minorities after partition.
Explanation: Signed by Nehru and Liaquat Ali Khan, it aimed to stop violence against Hindus and Muslims.

Q16. Define ‘Federalism’ in the Indian Constitution.

Answer: Federalism means power is divided between the central government and states in India.
Explanation: Both levels have their own roles, like the center handling defense and states managing education.

Q17. What is the role of the UN Security Council?

Answer: The UN Security Council works to maintain peace and can send troops or impose sanctions.
Explanation: It has 15 members, including 5 permanent ones with veto power.

Q18. What is ‘Global Warming’?

Answer: Global warming is the rise in Earth’s temperature due to pollution and greenhouse gases.
Explanation: It causes climate change, like melting ice and rising seas.

Section C: Short Answer Questions (4 Marks Each)

Q19. Explain the impact of the 1971 India-Pakistan War.

Answer: The 1971 war had these impacts:
1. Bangladesh Creation: East Pakistan became independent as Bangladesh.
2. India’s Strength: India showed its military power by defeating Pakistan.
3. Refugees: India helped millions of refugees from East Pakistan.
4. Global Role: India gained respect for supporting freedom.
Explanation: The war lasted 13 days and changed South Asia’s map.

Q20. What are the challenges to India’s permanent seat in the UN Security Council?

Answer: Challenges include:
1. Opposition: Countries like China and Pakistan don’t support India.
2. Veto Power: Current permanent members may not want to share power.
3. Reforms: UN rules are hard to change.
4. Competition: Other countries like Japan and Germany also want seats.
Explanation: India is pushing for a seat due to its size and global role.

Q21. How did the 1991 economic reforms change India?

Answer: The 1991 reforms:
1. Opened Economy: Allowed foreign companies to invest in India.
2. Growth: Increased jobs and industries like IT.
3. Trade: More imports and exports with the world.
4. Privatization: Reduced government control over businesses.
Explanation: Led by PM Narasimha Rao, these changes made India a global player.

Q22. Why is SAARC important for South Asia?

Answer: SAARC is important because:
1. Cooperation: Helps countries like India and Pakistan work together.
2. Trade: Boosts business between members.
3. Peace: Reduces tensions in the region.
4. Development: Focuses on poverty and education.
Explanation: Formed in 1985, SAARC unites South Asia despite challenges.

Q23. What are the effects of social media on Indian democracy?

Answer: Effects include:
1. Awareness: People learn about rights and issues.
2. Campaigns: Parties use it to reach voters.
3. Fake News: Spreads rumors that confuse people.
4. Participation: Citizens voice opinions online.
Explanation: Social media is powerful but has both good and bad sides.

Section D: Passage/Map-Based Questions (4 Marks Each)

Q24. Read the passage and answer:
India tested its nuclear weapons in 1998 under Operation Shakti. This made India a nuclear power but also caused global concern.
a) Where was the nuclear test conducted? (2 marks)
b) Why did some countries oppose it? (2 marks)

Answer:
a) Pokhran, Rajasthan.
b) They feared it would start an arms race and threaten peace.
Explanation: The 1998 tests showed India’s strength but worried nations like the USA.

Q25. On the map of India, identify and name:
a) The state where the first nuclear test (1974) happened. (2 marks)
b) The state with the longest international border with Bangladesh. (2 marks)

Answer:
a) Rajasthan (Pokhran, 1974 test).
b) West Bengal (shares a long border with Bangladesh).
Explanation: Rajasthan is key for nuclear history, and West Bengal has trade ties with Bangladesh.

Q26. Read the passage and answer:
The Paris Agreement (2015) aims to fight climate change. India joined it and promised to use more renewable energy.
a) What is the main goal of the Paris Agreement? (2 marks)
b) Name one renewable energy source India is focusing on. (2 marks)

Answer:
a) To limit global warming to below 2°C.
b) Solar energy.
Explanation: India is building solar plants to meet its Paris goals.

Section E: Long Answer Questions (6 Marks Each)

Q27. Discuss India’s role in the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM).

Answer: India’s role in NAM includes:
1. Leadership: Nehru co-founded NAM in 1961 with Tito and Nasser.
2. Neutrality: Stayed out of USA-Soviet rivalry in the Cold War.
3. Voice for Poor: Spoke for developing countries’ rights.
4. Peace: Promoted solving disputes without war.
5. Unity: Brought Asian and African nations together.
6. Relevance: Still uses NAM to stay independent today.
Explanation: NAM made India a leader of smaller nations globally.

Q28. Explain the changes after the removal of Article 370 in 2019.

Answer: Changes after 2019:
1. No Special Status: Jammu and Kashmir lost its separate constitution.
2. Union Territories: Split into J&K and Ladakh, ruled by the center.
3. Laws: All Indian laws now apply there, like RTI.
4. Development: More focus on jobs and infrastructure.
5. Criticism: Some say it reduced local rights.
6. Security: Tighter control to stop terrorism.
Explanation: This was a big shift in India’s federal structure.

Q29. What are the pros and cons of globalization for India?

Answer:
Pros:
1. Jobs: More companies like Google create work.
2. Technology: Better phones and internet access.
3. Trade: India exports more goods like medicines.
Cons:
1. Inequality: Rich get richer, poor stay poor.
2. Local Loss: Small businesses struggle against big firms.
3. Culture: Western influence weakens traditions.
Explanation: Globalization helps India grow but also brings challenges.

Q30. How has India’s relation with the USA evolved since the Cold War?

Answer: India-USA relations evolved like this:
1. Cold War: India was neutral, closer to Soviet Union, USA supported Pakistan.
2. 1990s: Ties improved after economic reforms and nuclear tests.
3. 2000s: Nuclear deal in 2008 made them partners.
4. Now: Work together on defense, trade, and countering China.
5. Trade: USA is a top partner for India’s IT and goods.
6. Challenges: Some disagreements over trade and Russia ties remain.
Explanation: From distance to strong friendship, ties have grown.
