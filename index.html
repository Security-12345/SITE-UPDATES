History & Civics Quiz Answers

Accurate answers to your history and civics questions

1. Arrange the following in chronological order

(i) Formation of the European Economic Community (1957)
(ii) Establishment of ASEAN (1967)
(iii) Privatisation of Agriculture in China (1982)
(iv) India’s war with China (1962)

Answer: (D) (i), (iv), (ii), (iii)

Explanation: The correct chronological order is 1957 (EEC), 1962 (India-China War), 1967 (ASEAN), 1982 (Privatisation in China).

2. Which one of the following was the third largest economy of the world in 2017?

(A) Germany
(B) France
(C) South Korea
(D) Japan

Answer: (D)

Explanation: In 2017, Japan was the third-largest economy by nominal GDP, after the United States and China.

3. Match the terms with their meaning

(a) Terrorism → (ii) Political violence
(b) Disarmament → (iv) Giving up certain kind of weapons
(c) Alliance → (i) Coalition of countries
(d) Security → (iii) Absence of threats

Answer: (A)

Explanation: The terms are correctly matched with their meanings.

4. Assertion (A) and Reason (R)

Assertion (A): Health epidemics such as HIV-AIDS, Bird Flu, and Coronavirus are the new threats to security.
Reason (R): Security goes beyond military threats to include a wide range of threats affecting human life.

Answer: (A)

Explanation: Both the assertion and reason are true, and the reason correctly explains the assertion.

5. Which one of the following options is not a cause of globalisation?

(A) Flow of capital
(B) Technological advancement
(C) Poverty of the underdeveloped countries
(D) Flow of ideas

Answer: (C)

Explanation: Poverty is a consequence of globalization, not a cause, unlike the other options.

6. In which country was the first meeting of the World Social Forum (WSF) held in 2001?

(A) Brazil
(B) South Africa
(C) Japan
(D) Italy

Answer: (A)

Explanation: The first WSF meeting was held in Porto Alegre, Brazil, in 2001.

7. Choose the name of the political party that ruled Jammu and Kashmir from 1952 to 1957.

(A) Indian National Congress
(B) National Conference
(C) Swatantra Party
(D) People’s Democratic Front

Answer: (B)

Explanation: The National Conference, led by Sheikh Abdullah, ruled Jammu and Kashmir during this period.

8. Who was the founder of Bharatiya Jana Sangh?

(A) Atal Bihari Vajpayee
(B) Deen Dayal Upadhyaya
(C) Balraj Madhok
(D) Shyama Prasad Mukherjee

Answer: (D)

Explanation: Shyama Prasad Mukherjee founded the Bharatiya Jana Sangh in 1951.

9. Which one of the following is the correct full form of NITI Aayog?

(A) National Institute of Technological Innovation
(B) National Institute for Transforming India
(C) New Institute of Technology and Information
(D) National Institute of Training India

Answer: (B)

Explanation: NITI Aayog stands for National Institution for Transforming India.

10. Who among the following was the Chairperson of the Planning Commission?

(A) President of India
(B) Prime Minister of India
(C) Minister of Planning
(D) Home Minister

Answer: (B)

Explanation: The Prime Minister of India was the ex-officio Chairperson of the Planning Commission.

11. Choose the correct name of the alliance made by the Indian National Congress in the elections of 2004.

(A) National Democratic Alliance
(B) United Progressive Alliance
(C) National Front
(D) United Front

Answer: (B)

Explanation: The Indian National Congress formed the United Progressive Alliance (UPA) in 2004.

12. Which one of the following political parties embraced ‘Gandhian Socialism’ as its ideology?

(A) Bharatiya Janata Party
(B) Communist Party of India
(C) National Conference
(D) Socialist Party

Answer: (D)

Explanation: The Socialist Party in India embraced Gandhian Socialism, focusing on decentralization and self-reliance.
